# General Health Advisory

> Module for ZEV AI coaching knowledge base

## Important Disclaimer

This module provides general wellness guidance based on wearable data patterns. It is NOT medical advice. Always consult a healthcare professional for medical concerns. ZEV coaches should preface health-related advice with appropriate caveats.

## When Metrics + Symptoms Suggest Illness

### Early Warning Signs in Your Data

Before you feel sick, your wearable data often shows changes:

- **Resting HR elevated 5-10+ bpm above baseline** for 2+ days
- **HRV dropping significantly** (15%+ below your 7-day average)
- **Body Battery not recovering** — fails to charge above 50 overnight
- **Sleep disrupted** — more awake time, less deep sleep than normal
- **Elevated overnight stress score** without obvious cause

### The "Am I Getting Sick?" Decision Tree

**If you notice elevated HR + low HRV + any of these symptoms:**

| Symptoms | Recommendation |
|----------|---------------|
| Mild scratchy throat, slight fatigue | Train light (50% intensity). Hydrate. Extra sleep. Monitor tomorrow. |
| Sore throat + body aches + fatigue | Skip training. Rest. Hydrate. Vitamin C, zinc. Monitor for 24-48h. |
| Fever (any degree) | No training until fever-free for 24 hours WITHOUT medication. |
| Chest congestion, breathing difficulty | No training. See a doctor if symptoms persist 3+ days. |
| Above-the-neck only (runny nose, mild congestion) | Light training OK if you feel up to it. Keep it zone 1-2 effort. |
| Below-the-neck symptoms (chest, body aches, GI) | No training. Full rest. See doctor if worsening. |

### The "Neck Check" Rule

A simple guideline for training when mildly unwell:
- **Symptoms above the neck** (runny nose, sneezing, mild sore throat): Light exercise is generally OK
- **Symptoms below the neck** (chest congestion, body aches, fever, GI issues): Rest completely

## Heart Rate Anomalies to Watch

### Patterns That Are Normal

- HR spikes during caffeine consumption (up to 10-15 bpm)
- Elevated HR after alcohol (can last 12-24 hours)
- Higher HR during heat/humidity
- Slightly elevated HR during menstrual cycle phases
- Morning HR 5-10 bpm higher than overnight low

### Patterns to Monitor

- **Consistently elevated resting HR** (10+ bpm above baseline for a week+) without obvious cause — could indicate overtraining, chronic stress, or subclinical illness
- **HR doesn't come down after exercise** — if heart rate stays elevated 30+ minutes post-workout at rest, you may have overtrained that session
- **Irregular rhythm notifications** from your wearable — worth mentioning to your GP at next visit

### Patterns to Act On (See a Doctor)

- **Resting HR suddenly 20+ bpm above normal** without fever or obvious cause
- **Heart rate spikes at rest** for no reason (anxiety, palpitations)
- **Chest pain during exercise** — stop immediately
- **Dizziness or fainting during exercise** — stop, seek medical attention

## Blood Oxygen (SpO2) Guidelines

- **95-100%:** Normal range. No concerns.
- **92-95%:** Slightly low but can be normal during deep sleep or at altitude. If consistent during the day, worth monitoring.
- **Below 92%:** Consult a doctor, especially if combined with breathing difficulty or fatigue.
- **Overnight dips below 90%:** Could indicate sleep apnea. Worth discussing with your doctor.

**Note:** Wrist-based SpO2 is less accurate than medical pulse oximeters. Use it for trends, not absolute values.

## Stress Score Interpretation

- **0-25 (Rest):** Parasympathetic dominant. Recovery state. Good.
- **26-50 (Low):** Mild stress. Normal for light activity or focused work.
- **51-75 (Medium):** Moderate stress. Normal during work or moderate exercise. Concerning if sustained all day.
- **76-100 (High):** Significant stress. Normal during intense exercise. If happening at rest, something is wrong.

### When High Stress Should Prompt Action

- **High stress during sleep:** Sleep quality is severely compromised. Check environment, caffeine timing, screen habits.
- **High stress all day without exercise:** Chronic stress response. Consider: workload, emotional stress, dehydration, illness.
- **Stress never drops below 50:** The body isn't entering recovery states. Prioritize: sleep, breathing exercises, reduce stimulants.

## Hydration and Training

### General Guidelines
- **Daily baseline:** 35ml per kg of body weight (for a 90kg person: ~3.2 liters)
- **Add for exercise:** 500-750ml per hour of moderate exercise
- **Add for heat:** Extra 500ml on hot days
- **Add for sauna:** 500ml per 15-20 minute session

### Signs of Dehydration in Your Data
- Elevated resting heart rate
- Lower HRV than normal
- Darker urine (check before training)
- Poor Body Battery recovery

## When to Share Data with Your Doctor

Your ZEV data can be valuable context for your GP. Consider sharing when:

- **Annual checkup:** Show resting HR trends, sleep patterns, activity levels
- **Feeling unwell:** Show the week of data leading up to symptoms
- **Heart concerns:** Share HR data showing anomalies
- **Sleep issues:** Show sleep stage breakdown and SpO2 overnight
- **Mental health check-in:** Show stress trends and Body Battery patterns

**How to frame it:** "My fitness tracker has been showing [specific pattern]. I wanted to bring it up in case it's relevant." Doctors appreciate data-informed patients, but present it as context, not self-diagnosis.

## Supplements and Recovery Basics

### Evidence-Based Supplements

| Supplement | Benefit | Dosage | Notes |
|-----------|---------|--------|-------|
| Creatine monohydrate | Strength, recovery, brain function | 5g daily | Most researched supplement. Safe long-term. |
| Vitamin D | Bone health, immune function, mood | 1000-4000 IU daily | Most people are deficient. Test levels if unsure. |
| Omega-3 (fish oil) | Inflammation, heart health | 1-3g EPA+DHA daily | From diet or supplement. |
| Magnesium | Sleep, muscle recovery, stress | 200-400mg before bed | Glycinate or threonate forms best absorbed. |
| Protein powder | Convenient protein source | As needed to hit daily target | Whey, casein, or plant-based. Not magic, just convenient. |

### Skip These (Waste of Money for Most People)
- BCAAs (if you eat enough protein)
- Fat burners / thermogenics
- Testosterone boosters
- Most "pre-workout" blends (just have caffeine)
- Detox teas/cleanses
